# LinkedIn Profile Builder

## Core principle

LinkedIn is not a CV. It's a positioning tool.

A CV lists what you did. A LinkedIn profile answers: **who do you help, with what, and why should they trust you?**

The algorithm favors profiles that are clear, specific, and active. The reader (recruiter, client, partner, peer) decides in 8 seconds whether to keep reading.

---

## The dual-identity pattern

Many professionals have two simultaneous identities: a corporate role (credibility anchor) and a personal brand (entrepreneur, trainer, consultant, builder). The challenge is to present both without one diluting the other.

**The formula:** Lead with what makes you distinctive (usually the personal brand or the combination), use the corporate role as the credibility proof, not the identity.

| Wrong order | Right order |
|---|---|
| "E-Merch Manager at [Company] \| Also interested in AI" | "AI Apps Builder \| Founder @ [Startup] \| 17 years in retail at [Company]" |
| The company defines you | You define yourself; the company backs you up |

## Headline

The headline is the most-read element after your name. It appears in search results, comment sections, connection requests — everywhere.

**Formula A — Portuguese market, training/community angle:**
```
[Primary expertise] | [Secondary expertise] | [Corporate role @ Brand] | [Trainer/Formador] | [N alunos/clientes] | [Community or project metric]
```

**Formula B — International/executive angle (English):**
```
Helping [specific audience] [achieve specific outcome] | [Role] @ [Company] | [Credential or differentiator]
```

**Formula C — Founder + corporate hybrid:**
```
[Domain] | Founder @ [Company] — [one-line what it does] | [Corporate role] | [Proof point]
```

**Rules:**
- Max 220 characters (LinkedIn truncates after ~120 in search)
- Include at least one number or metric if you have one
- Avoid generic words: "passionate", "experienced", "dynamic", "results-oriented"
- Keywords matter for search: include the terms your target audience actually searches

## About / Summary

This is the highest-value real estate on your profile. 2,600 characters maximum. Most people waste it on a CV summary.

**Structure that works:**

```
[Opening hook — a tension, insight, or bold statement. NOT "I am a..."]

[What you do and who you help — 1-2 sentences, specific]

[Corporate role as proof — 1 paragraph: what you lead, what you've built, scale/impact]

[Side venture / training / community — 1 paragraph: what it is, who it's for, metrics if available]

[What makes you different — "O que me distingue?" / "What sets me apart:" — 2-3 specific differentiators]

[Call to action — one clear sentence: what you want the reader to do next]
```

**Opening hook patterns:**
- The tension: "Most [professionals] invest in [X]. Few actually [outcome]."
- The paradox: "I am not a [tech person] who learned business. I am a [business person] who learned to build [tech]."
- The mission: "Ajudo [specific audience] a [specific outcome] — without [common frustration]."

**Tone:**
- Portuguese profiles → conversational PT-PT, first person, direct
- International profiles → English, slightly more formal, problem-solution framing
- Avoid: passive voice, bullet lists of skills (those belong in the Skills section), vague adjectives

## Experience descriptions

Most people list responsibilities. Recruiters and clients want outcomes.

**Before (responsibilities):**
```
- Managed product portfolio across multiple categories
- Coordinated teams and agencies
- Developed e-commerce strategy
```

**After (outcomes + context):**
```
Led the e-commerce and category strategy for [X categories] in a market where [context].
Built [what] from [baseline] to [result]. Managed a team of [N] and [key achievement].
```

**Formula per role:**
1. Context sentence: what the role was, at what scale
2. What you built or transformed (not just managed)
3. One specific result with a number if possible
4. What you learned or what makes this role relevant to what comes next

## Featured section

Use this to anchor your personal brand with tangible proof:

| What to feature | Why |
|---|---|
| Company/project website | Shows you build real things |
| LinkedIn article you wrote | Positions you as a thinker in your domain |
| Video of a talk or class | Shows you can communicate |
| Case study or portfolio PDF | Makes the abstract concrete |
| Link to a community or newsletter | Shows audience/reach |

Order: most impressive first. One dead link destroys credibility.

## Content strategy

Posting on LinkedIn compounds over time. The goal is not viral posts — it's consistent positioning.

**The 3 content types:**

| Type | Frequency | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Insight** — your take on something in your domain | 2-3x/week | Builds authority |
| **Behind-the-scenes** — what you're building, learning, testing | 1x/week | Builds trust |
| **Proof** — results, launches, testimonials, case studies | As they happen | Builds credibility |

**Post format that works on LinkedIn:**
```
[Hook — first line that stops the scroll. One sentence. Specific, not vague.]

[Context — why this matters. 2-3 sentences.]

[Substance — the insight, the lesson, the result. Use short paragraphs or numbered list.]

[Closing — question to invite comments, or a CTA]

[Hashtags — 3-5, specific to your domain, at the bottom]
```

**What kills reach:** Too many hashtags (>10), posting and ghosting (no replies to comments), purely promotional posts, walls of text.

**What builds reach:** First-person stories, specific numbers, contrarian takes backed by evidence, replies to your own comments in the first hour.

## Dual identity — how to present corporate + entrepreneur

| Situation | Approach |
|---|---|
| Employer knows and supports | Mention both openly in headline and About |
| Employer knows but is neutral | Feature startup in About, not headline |
| Separate audiences | Use same profile but calibrate content mix |

**Connecting the two:** The best positioning makes the corporate role and the personal brand reinforce each other. Example: "I run digital transformation at [major company] — and I build the tools I wish existed."

## Profile audit checklist

Before finalizing any profile, check:

- [ ] Headline includes at least one specific metric or differentiator
- [ ] About opens with a hook (not "I am" or "With X years of experience")
- [ ] About mentions who you help and what outcome you deliver
- [ ] Featured section has at least one live link
- [ ] Current role description focuses on outcomes, not just responsibilities
- [ ] Skills section (top 3) matches what you want to be known for
- [ ] Profile photo is professional (face visible, good light, approachable)
- [ ] Banner image reinforces your brand or current focus
- [ ] Custom URL is set (linkedin.com/in/your-name)
- [ ] Contact info is up to date
