# AI Video Creator

Two complementary tools for producing high-quality video content from static assets,
with no camera, no crew, no studio.

---

## Tool 1 — Flow: Cinematic Ads from Images + Storyboard

Flow creates 15–30 second video ads from product photos and a text storyboard.
Output quality: cinematic / broadcast-ready.

### 4-step workflow

1. **Prepare images** — photograph the product or service you want to advertise.
   Clean background, good lighting. 3–5 images is enough.

2. **Write the storyboard** — describe in plain text what should happen in the video:
   ```
   Example: "The cat walks slowly toward the bowl, sniffs the food,
   eats contentedly, lies down satisfied. Product appears in close-up
   with voiceover."
   ```

3. **Generate the video** — Flow combines your images with the storyboard and
   produces a video with fluid motion, transitions, and background music.

4. **Add voiceover** — add an AI-generated professional narrator voice with
   the product name and sales message.

### Storyboard prompt template
```
Scene 1: [setting / atmosphere / camera angle]
Action: [what happens — subject movement, product reveal]
Scene 2: [transition or cut]
Action: [product highlight / CTA moment]
Voiceover: "[product name] — [tagline / key benefit]"
Duration: [15s / 30s]
Tone: [cinematic / energetic / calm / premium]
```

### When to use Flow
- Product launch ads for social media (Instagram, Facebook, TikTok)
- Service promotion (clinic, restaurant, real estate)
- Any business that needs TV-quality video with no production budget

## Tool 2 — Higgsfield: Animate Static Photos

Higgsfield transforms static photographs into realistic animated videos.
The photo becomes a short video clip with natural movement.

### Use cases

| Input | Output |
|---|---|
| Car parked on road | Car driving forward, dust and movement |
| Boat in harbor | Boat rocking on waves, water movement |
| Product on shelf | Product rotating, light play |
| Landscape photo | Wind in trees, moving clouds, flowing water |
| Portrait photo | Subtle head movement, blinking, hair movement |

### Workflow
1. Upload the static photo
2. Describe the movement you want: *"the car moves forward slowly, dust rising"*
3. Higgsfield generates a 3–8 second realistic animation
4. Use the clip standalone or feed it into Flow as a scene

### Prompt template for Higgsfield
```
Animate this photo: [brief description of what's in the image]
Movement: [what should move and how — slow/fast, direction]
Atmosphere: [cinematic / natural / dramatic]
Duration: [3s / 5s / 8s]
```

---

## Combined workflow: Higgsfield → Flow

For maximum quality, combine both tools:

1. **Higgsfield** — animate your product/service photos into short clips
2. **Flow** — use those clips as scenes, add storyboard narrative + voiceover
3. Result: a fully produced cinematic ad from photographs alone

```
Total time: 20–40 minutes
Cost: fraction of traditional video production
Equipment needed: smartphone camera
```

## Practical examples

### Product ad (e-commerce)
```
Images: 4 product photos (front, detail, in-use, packaging)
Storyboard: "Product appears on clean surface, rotates slowly,
hand picks it up, uses it, satisfied expression. Logo end card."
Voiceover: "[Product name] — [key benefit]. Available at [store]."
```

### Service ad (clinic / restaurant / gym)
```
Images: interior, team, before/after or dish/equipment
Storyboard: "Client arrives, is welcomed, receives service,
leaves smiling. Space shown in best light."
Voiceover: "[Business name] — [what makes you different]. Book now."
```

### Social media content (Instagram / LinkedIn)
```
Images: 1–2 photos of product or behind-the-scenes
Higgsfield: animate the hero image
Flow: add text overlay and music
Format: 15s loop for Instagram Reels / TikTok
```

## Quality rules

- **Images**: minimum 1080px, good lighting, clean composition
- **Storyboard**: be specific about movement — vague descriptions produce generic results
- **Voiceover**: shorter is better — 1 sentence per 10 seconds of video
- **Cross-check**: if information is critical, verify with a second source (Manus, web search)

## When NOT to use these tools

| Situation | Better approach |
|---|---|
| Existing video footage to edit | Traditional editing (Premiere, CapCut) |
| Long-form content (>2 min) | Traditional production |
| Real person speaking to camera | Record directly — AI avatars feel less authentic |
| Screen recording / tutorial | Loom, OBS |
